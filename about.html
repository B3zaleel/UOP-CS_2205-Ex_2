About Me

I'm Nagato Uzumaki. I live in Edinburgh, Scotland with my wife, two dogs, two children and a lot of computers. After playing around with C#, I picked up JavaScript, Python, R, HTML, CSS, Blender, Inkscape. I am currently learning Machine Learning and when I decide to take a break, I work on polishing my animation with Blender. In the future, I plan on picking up LISP since I find its design fascinating.

Currently, I would consider myself a reasonably experienced windows and web software developer. However, I am always ready to broaden my horizon if the horizon promises greener pastures. Computers are really powerful machines, but are designed based on the people who use them and by those who create them. Despite that, a lot of people who design these machines and the programs that are run inside them usually forget those they make it for. Although we know we can write code to do a lot of different things, design is also very important to get people to see the beauty in your work and maybe forget the hard work you put into it for a moment or longer. This is why I tend to focus not only on programming in this blog, but also on graphics design.

Why do you blog?

I started this blog in 2007 as way for me to keep track of my thoughts, ideas, and what I had learned over the years. Depending on how far my research on what I'm currently working on takes me, I could document my research findings with a blog post here on this site. I also read some books when I have some free time at hand and they're not limited to the non-fiction genre but include fiction as well. Books like Crime and Punishment aren't non-fiction but have a worthy lesson to be learned from reading it. Thus when I do read those books, I pick out key points from them and make a short review of them for my readers to learn from what I have read. I hope others will find my posts helpful, inspiring or interesting. I am always welcome to receive emails as long as they're on topic.
